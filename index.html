10th Workshop on Automated Knowledge Base Construction

While Large Language Models (LLMs) have revolutionized NLP, they remain prone to hallucinations, reasoning “mode-collapse” in open-ended generation, and a lack of factual provenance. The Automated Knowledge Base Construction (AKBC) workshop addresses a key missing piece of the generative era: structured knowledge. Knowledge Bases (KBs) serve as ground truth for fact verification, the semantic backbone for constrained decoding in generation, and as a resource behind Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG).

The workshop is co-located with EMNLP 2026 in Budapest, Hungary. It follows a successful series of previous editions: as an independent conference in 2022, 2021, and 2020, as workshop in 2017 at NIPS, in 2016 at NAACL, in 2014 at NIPS, in 2013 at CIKM, in 2012 at NAACL, and in 2010 as a stand-alone event in Grenoble, France.

Call for Papers

The Automated Knowledge Base Construction (AKBC) workshop returns in 2026, bringing together researchers and practitioners working on the construction, integration, and use of structured knowledge in the era of large language models (LLMs). As LLMs continue to transform NLP, challenges such as hallucinations, lack of provenance, and limited reasoning reliability highlight the need for robust, structured knowledge representations.

AKBC provides a venue at the intersection of natural language processing, knowledge representation, databases, and machine learning, with a particular focus on the interaction between symbolic knowledge and generative models.

We invite submissions on topics including, but not limited to:

  • Knowledge extraction and consolidation from text and multimodal data
  • Knowledge graphs, ontologies, and schema alignment
  • Graph-based and structured question answering
  • Reasoning, verification, and factuality in knowledge-intensive systems
  • Neuro-symbolic methods and structured grounding of LLMs
  • Knowledge in and about LLMs (extraction, editing, analysis, and evaluation)

Submission Types

We invite three types of submissions:

1. Regular Research Papers (via ARR)

We accept papers reviewed through the ACL Rolling Review (ARR). Authors can submit papers to ARR and commit them to AKBC. These papers should present original research contributions and will follow standard ACL/EMNLP reviewing policies.

2. Vision Papers (direct submission)

We invite vision papers that articulate emerging research directions, challenges, or unifying perspectives for knowledge base construction in the age of LLMs. These papers should stimulate discussion and outline promising future avenues rather than focus solely on completed empirical results.

3. Shared Task Papers (direct submission)

We invite system description and analysis papers for the LM-KBC shared task, co-located with the workshop. Submissions should describe participating systems, methodologies, and insights gained from the challenge.

Format and Presentation

Accepted papers will be presented as posters, with a selection invited for lightning talks. The workshop will also feature invited keynotes from leading researchers in academia and industry.
